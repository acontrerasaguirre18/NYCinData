Systemic Oppression
The New York City Police Department's policies, practices, and culture perpetuate discrimination and bias against racial and ethnic minority communities, resulting in unequal treatment and disproportionate impacts on these communities. Historically, the NYPD has taken a hostile approach towards interacting with communities of color. The department has employed discriminatory practices, including racial profiling, excessive use of force, and abuse of power, that disproportionately affect Black, Hispanic, and other minority communities in NYC.
Racial
Profiling
Racial profiling by police in NYC refers to the practice of law enforcement officers targeting individuals for suspicion or scrutiny based on their perceived race or ethnicity, rather than on their behavior or evidence of wrongdoing. Racial profiling can result in disproportionate targeting of individuals from specific racial or ethnic groups for stops, searches, arrests, or other forms of police action. There have been numerous reports and studies that suggest racial profiling is a pervasive issue in NYC, particularly affecting communities of color. The (NYPD) has faced significant scrutiny and criticism for its practices related to racial profiling.
Disproportionate Arrests of People
of Color
Individuals from Black and Hispanic communities are arrested and incarcerated at higher rates compared to individuals from White and Asian communities, despite similar rates of criminal activity. This pattern of disproportionate arrests is often attributed to systemic biases within the criminal justice system, including racial profiling, discriminatory policing practices, and disparities in access to legal representation and other resources.
